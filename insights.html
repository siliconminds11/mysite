Executive thought leadership on Digital Trust.

Perspective for principals and boards navigating a reputation landscape reshaped by AI — not SEO advice, and not marketing content.

AI & Reputation

Why AI Platforms Are Now the First Interview

Long before a candidate, advisor, or partner sits across the table, a growing share of decision-makers have already consulted an AI platform for a summary of who that person is. This piece examines how large language models have become a diligence layer that precedes — and often shapes — the human conversation that follows, and what that means for how reputation must now be managed.

Entity Authority

The Knowledge Graph as a Strategic Asset

Structured entity data has quietly become as consequential as narrative reputation. When a name is not clearly and precisely represented in the Knowledge Graph, search engines and AI platforms are forced to infer identity — and inference introduces error. This piece explains why entity precision now belongs on the same agenda as brand and communications strategy.

Methodology

Measuring What Used to Be Unmeasurable

Reputation has historically resisted quantification — treated as a qualitative impression rather than a measurable asset. This piece walks through the logic of the Digital Trust Framework™: why seven pillars, why these particular weights, and how a single score can meaningfully summarize a complex, cross-platform reality.

Executive Trust

The Credential Gap Most Executives Don't Know They Have

Even accomplished, well-documented professionals frequently carry a quiet gap between their actual credentials and how those credentials are represented online — a fragmented name, a missing registration number, an unconfirmed affiliation. This piece explains why these small gaps matter disproportionately to how AI platforms assess authority.

Risk

Reputation Risk Rarely Arrives as a Single Event

Crisis narratives suggest reputation damage is sudden. In practice, most Digital Trust erosion is gradual — small inconsistencies compounding quietly over years. This piece examines why continuous monitoring, not episodic response, is the more strategic posture.

Industry Focus

Why Healthcare Reputation Now Runs Through AI Platforms

Patients increasingly consult AI platforms alongside — or instead of — traditional search when evaluating a physician or institution. This piece looks at what that shift means for how credentials and affiliations must be documented and maintained.
